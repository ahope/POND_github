We’re counting calories from saturated fat, which are found in foods from animals, such as meat and dairy products. ½ a chicken breast with skin contains about 2 points of saturated fat, while nonfat yogurt or skim milk has none.

Major sources of saturated fatty acids in the American diet include regular (full-fat) cheese (9% of total saturated fat intake); pizza (6%); grain-based desserts48 (6%); dairy-based desserts49 (6%); chicken and chicken mixed dishes (6%); and sausage, franks, bacon, and ribs (5%) (Figure 3-4).
