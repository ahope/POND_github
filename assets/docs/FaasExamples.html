Fats, Alcohol, or Added Sugars
Common solid fats:
  • Fats that are solid at room temperature
  • butter
  • stick margarine
  • shortening
Foods high in solid fats include:
  • many cheeses
  • creams
  • ice creams
  • well-marbled cuts of meats
  • regular ground beef
  • bacon
  • sausages
  • poultry skin
  • many baked goods (such as cookies, crackers, donuts, pastries, and croissants)

Alcohol

  • beer
  • wine
  • liquor
Common foods with added sugars are:
  • regular soft drinks
  • candy
  • cakes
  • cookies
  • pies
  • fruit drinks, such as fruitades and fruit punch
  • milk-based desserts and products, such as ice cream, sweetened yogurt and sweetened milk
  • grain products such as sweet rolls and cinnamon toast
Names for added sugars on food labels include:
  • brown sugar
  • corn sweetener
  • corn syrup
  • dextrose
  • fructose
  • fruit juice concentrates
  • glucose
  • high-fructose corn syrup
  • honey
  • invert sugar
  • lactose
  • maltose
  • malt syrup
  • molasses
  • raw sugar
  • sucrose
  • sugar
  • syrup
